# Relay — Support

Relay is a universal remote for smart TVs and streaming devices on your home Wi-Fi. This page is the
support URL for the App Store listing.

## Getting started

1. Open Relay and tap **Find devices**.
2. When asked, allow **Local Network** access — Relay needs it to find TVs on your Wi-Fi. Nothing
   leaves your home.
3. Pick your device from the list and follow the on-screen pairing steps.
4. Assign it to a room, and you're in the remote.

No account and no sign-up are required.

## If no devices are found

- Make sure your iPhone and your TV are on the **same Wi-Fi network**.
- Turn off any **VPN** temporarily.
- **Guest or "isolated" Wi-Fi networks** block device discovery — use your main network.
- Some TVs need **network control enabled** in their own settings.
- Tap **Rescan**, or **Add manually** by brand and IP address.

## Which TVs work

Relay controls devices over your network — iPhones have no infrared, so it is not an IR remote.
Support depends on the TV's own network features. The in-app **Compatibility** page (Settings ▸
Compatibility) lists, in plain language, what each brand and each of your paired devices can do.

Apple TV is not controllable by third-party apps due to Apple's platform restrictions, and Fire TV
support is experimental. Relay tells you this honestly rather than pretending otherwise.

## Something not working?

- Open the device's **Reliability Center** (the ⓘ on the remote screen) for its live status,
  latency, supported controls, and plain-English troubleshooting.
- On the beta? Use **Settings ▸ About & Support ▸ Send Beta Feedback** to report your TV model and
  which commands work — it helps us prioritize fixes by device.
